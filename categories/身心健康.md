## 1. 心智健康日记引导

**Prompt：**
```
Help me process my emotions through journaling.

Today I'm feeling: [描述当前情绪]
What happened: [简要描述事件]
My automatic thoughts: [第一时间冒出的想法]

Guide me through:
1. What evidence supports this feeling? What evidence contradicts it?
2. Is there a more balanced way to view this situation?
3. What would I tell a friend who was in the same situation?
4. What's one small action I can take to shift my state?
5. What did I learn about myself today?

Be compassionate but not saccharine. Don't just validate — help me reframe.
```

> 用法：认知行为疗法(CBT)导向的日记引导，帮助重构负面思维模式。

## 2. 焦虑管理工具包

**Prompt：**
```
I'm feeling anxious about [具体事情]. Help me use evidence-based techniques to manage this.

Guide me through:
1. Grounding exercise (5-4-3-2-1 technique: 5 things I see, 4 I feel, 3 I hear, 2 I smell, 1 I taste)
2. Cognitive reframing: what's the worst that could happen? What's most likely? What's best?
3. Circle of control: what's within my control vs. outside it?
4. One actionable step I can take right now
5. Self-compassion statement tailored to this situation

Use a calm, warm tone. Don't minimize the feeling.
```
> 📅 2026-07-11
> 来源：MyFlourish.ai / Nailedit.ai — Mental Health Prompts (2026)
> 用法：结合grounding、认知重构和控制圈划分的焦虑管理工具包。

## 4. 自我关怀实践

**Prompt：**
```
Help me practice self-compassion.

Situation: [描述让你自我批评的事]
My inner critic says: [内心批评的声音]

Guide me through Dr. Kristin Neff's three components of self-compassion:
1. Mindfulness — Acknowledge the pain without suppressing or exaggerating it
2. Common humanity — Remind me that struggle is universal, not isolating
3. Self-kindness — What would I say to a dear friend in this situation?

End with a self-compassion break: a short phrase I can say to myself right now.
```

## 5. 感恩日记练习

**Prompt：**
```
Guide me through a gratitude journaling session.

Today's general mood: [今天的心情]
Something good that happened: [好事]
Something challenging: [有挑战的事]
Time available: [5/10/15 minutes]

Lead the session:

1. Three good things — help me identify 3 specific positive moments from today (not generic, not big — small and specific)
2. Why they happened — who or what contributed to each? What role did I play?
3. Reframing challenge — one challenge from today and what it taught me or how it made me stronger
4. Appreciation for someone — who am I grateful for today and why? (Prompt me to consider telling them)
5. Forward-looking gratitude — something I'm looking forward to tomorrow

Keep the tone warm but not saccharine. Don't force positivity — acknowledge difficult emotions.
```
> 📅 2026-07-12
> 来源：Positive Psychology / AI Wellness (2026)
> 用法：基于积极心理学研究的感恩练习——三件好事法，关注具体事件而不只是笼统感谢。

## 6. 社交焦虑应对策略

**Prompt：**
```
I feel socially anxious about [具体社交场景]. Help me prepare.

Event: [活动/会议/聚会/演讲]
Who will be there: [在场的人]
What triggers my anxiety: [具体触发因素]
Physical symptoms: [身体反应 — 心跳加速/出汗/声音发抖]
What I usually do: [平时如何应对]

Provide:

1. Pre-event preparation — mental rehearsal, breathing technique, mantra
2. Grounding technique for the moment — a 10-second exercise I can do discreetly
3. Conversation starters — 5 low-pressure openers relevant to this event
4. Exit strategy — how to leave gracefully if overwhelmed
5. Cognitive reframing — challenge the anxious thoughts with balanced alternatives
6. Post-event reflection — questions to ask myself afterward (not just what went wrong)
7. Progressive exposure — smaller steps I could take to build confidence over time

Be practical, not platitudinous. No "just be confident" or "nobody's judging you."
```
> 📅 2026-07-12
> 来源：Anxiety Management / AI Mental Health (2026)
> 用法：社交焦虑应对工具包——事前的心理排练、事中的落地技巧、事后的反思框架。

Sleep quality: [感觉恢复了吗?]

6. Wake-up routine — light exposure, temperature, morning protocol for better next night
7. Tracking — what to log to identify patterns (not obsessive, just informative)
8. When to seek professional help — red flags for sleep disorders

Provide a "tonight's plan" — a one-page summary I can follow right now.

## 8. 情绪调节工具箱

**Prompt：**
```
I'm feeling [当前情绪]。Help me regulate it.

Emotion: [愤怒/悲伤/焦虑/麻木/崩溃]
Trigger: [触发事件]
Intensity (1-10): [强度]
Available time: [有多少时间处理]
Environment: [我在哪里 — 家/办公室/公共场所]

Give me an emotion regulation toolkit:

1. Name it to tame it — help me label the exact emotion (beyond "stressed")
2. Immediate 5-minute intervention — something I can do right now, where I am
3. Physical regulation — body-based techniques (breathing, movement, temperature)
4. Cognitive reappraisal — how else could I interpret this situation?
5. Opposite action — what would acting opposite to the emotion look like?
6. Values check — what matters more than this feeling right now?
7. Containment — if I can't resolve it now, how to set it aside for later

End with one question to check in with myself in 30 minutes.
Warning: Do NOT suggest anything that would be dangerous or irresponsible.
```
> 📅 2026-07-12
> 来源：Emotion Regulation / AI Mental Health (2026)
> 用法：情绪调节工具箱——基于DBT和CBT技术，分时间长度和场景可选，帮你从情绪中出来。

## 16. 正念日记与情绪反思

**Prompt：**
```
Guide me through a guided journaling session:

Current emotional state: [情绪描述]
Recent event: [最近影响你的事]
Goal: [理解情绪/找到解决方案/感恩练习]
Time available: [5/10/15 minutes]

Journaling prompts:
1. Check-in: Describe what you're feeling in 3 words (no judgment)
2. Body scan: Where do you feel this in your body?
3. The story: What happened, without interpretation (just facts)
4. The interpretation: What meaning am I assigning to this?
5. Reframing: Is there another way to see this?
6. Action: One small thing I can do right now

After I respond to each prompt, offer a brief reflection but don't fix anything. Focus on creating space, not solving problems.
```
> 来源：AI Academy — Mindfulness & Journaling 2026
> 用法：引导式正念日记，从情绪识别到重新框架到小行动承诺。

## 17. 人际关系沟通修复

**Prompt：**
```
Help me prepare for a difficult conversation with [人物关系]:

Context: [发生了什么]
My feelings: [我的感受]
Their likely perspective: [对方可能的视角]
My goal: [期望的结果]
Previous attempts: [以前试过什么]

Provide:
1. Non-violent communication (NVC) framework script:
   - Observation: What happened (facts, not judgment)
   - Feeling: How I feel about it
   - Need: What need is unmet
   - Request: Specific, actionable, negotiable request
2. Predicted responses and how to handle each
3. What NOT to say (trigger words, blame statements)
4. Body language and tone guidance
5. "I statement" alternatives for accusations
6. Repair attempts (when things get heated, how to de-escalate)

Focus on preserving the relationship, not winning the argument.
```
> 来源：AI Academy — Relationship Prompts 2026
> 用法：用非暴力沟通框架准备棘手对话，关注关系维护而非争胜负。---
